# Project Vision

## Project

Jeremy Laycock Portfolio

## Purpose

Design and build a modern, high-quality UX/UI and Product Design portfolio that demonstrates strategic thinking, design craft and measurable outcomes.

The portfolio should communicate not only what I designed, but how I think, solve problems and collaborate with teams.

## Primary Goal

Create one of the strongest UX/UI and Product Design portfolios in Australia for Senior UX/UI Designer and Senior Product Designer roles — including roles with practice-level design leadership (owning design within a squad, or across a small org, as demonstrated at ANZ and Goodwork) — but not people-management or formal Lead/team-lead roles.

The portfolio should maximise opportunities for interviews while accurately representing my skills, experience and approach.

## Target Audience

Primary audiences:

* Recruiters
* Hiring Managers
* Design Leaders
* Product Leaders

Secondary audiences:

* Designers
* Developers
* Product Managers
* Potential clients

## Source Material

My existing portfolio is the primary source of content, projects and case studies.

https://www.jeremylaycock.com.au/

Do not simply recreate the existing website.

Critically evaluate it and recommend better ways to present my work.

## Design Principles

The portfolio should communicate:

* thoughtful product thinking
* evidence-based UX
* high-quality UI craft
* accessibility
* strong interaction design
* systems thinking
* collaboration
* product strategy
* mobile-first thinking
* attention to detail

The overall experience should feel:

* modern
* confident
* understated
* premium
* professional
* highly considered

Prioritise clarity over cleverness.

Avoid unnecessary decoration, trends or visual clutter.

Every design decision should support the overall story.

## Success Criteria

The finished portfolio should:

* Clearly communicate my value within the first minute of viewing.
* Demonstrate strategic UX thinking rather than simply showcasing screens.
* Present case studies as compelling stories with measurable outcomes.
* Showcase strong visual design and interaction design.
* Be accessible and performant.
* Work exceptionally well across desktop, tablet and mobile.
* Reflect current best practice in UX, Product Design and front-end development.
* Leave hiring managers with confidence in my ability to lead complex digital design projects.

## Guiding Principles

Throughout this project:

* Challenge assumptions.
* Optimise for outcomes rather than convention.
* Support recommendations with evidence and reasoning.
* Consider both user needs and business goals.
* Prioritise long-term quality over short-term convenience.
* Aim to create a portfolio that remains relevant and effective for years to come.
